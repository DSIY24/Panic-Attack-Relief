What is a Panic Attack ?

A panic attack is a feeling of sudden and intense anxiety.

This often results in the following symptoms


  • Shaking
  • Breathlessness
  • Rapid ,Irregular heartbeats
  • Feeling disordered

The Starting Stages

If you have just felt the symptoms mentioned above you are most likely having a panic attack, you can manage your symptoms at the moment with strategies like deep breathing, mindfulness exercises, muscle relaxation and more...


Frist recognise that you are having a panic attack to take away the fear of impending doom or a heart attack which are both common symptoms of a panic attack. This allows you to focus on the techniques to reduce your symptoms mentioned below.


Utilize deep breathing, while hyperventilating is a symptom that can't cause any physical harm it, can increase fear.Inorder to prevent it deep breathing can be utilized during an attack.


Focus on taking a deep breath through your nose, feel the air slowly fill your chest and stomach. Then... slowly exhale through your mouth and feel the air leave your body. In between breathing in and out hold your breath for a count of 4 and repeat If you are able to control your breath you are less likely to experience hyperventilating that can make other more serious symptoms and the panic attack itself worse.

Persistent Symptoms

Most panic attacks last between 5 and 20 minutes. Some have been reported to last up to an hour.So if you have been feeling your symptoms for longer then there may be reason for concern


Both panic and heart attacks cause chest discomfort, but there is a difference. “With a heart attack, pain radiates to other areas like the arm, jaw or neck,” Dr. Miller says. “If it's a panic attack,” she notes, “pain will typically stay in the chest.”


As Dr Miller mentioned above, if the pain / discomfort does spread to other areas of the body and other unrelated symptoms arise it would be valid to seek medical intervention as soon as possible


On the other hand if you are sure that you are undergoing a panic attack and want to relive the symptoms,Benzodiazepines, such as alprazolam (Xanax), can help treat the symptoms of panic when they occur. However, they won't help treat an underlying anxiety disorder and can quickly lead to dependence.For this reason, doctors only recommend them for short-term use during a crisis.

How To Prepare

Going for counseling can often help people who have panic attacks and who have panic disorders. CBT (Cognative-behaviour therapy) aims to help you change the way you see challenging or frightening situations and to help you find new ways to approach these challenges as they arise. You can find CBT for individuals or groups, online or face-to-face, and the length of treatment can also vary. In exposure-based CBT, your therapist will expose you to something that can trigger a panic attack and help you work your way through it. As well as changing behavior, there is some evidence that CBT might affect structures in your brain that are responsible for panic symptoms.


Another effective method in diagnosing Panic Attacks is through the use of Heart Beat Sensors and Electrocardiographs
